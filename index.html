Born Between Two Worlds

The title born between two worlds captures Trevor Noah’s experience growing up between the worlds of black and and white in apartheid South Africa.. As a child of a black mother and white father his existence was illegal ,making him caught between racial and societal boundaries. The new title is a reflection of his identity struggles, sense of not fully belonging to one group and the complexities of growing up in a divided country.

Abel wanted a traditional marriage with a traditional wife … ‘He only wants a woman who is free because his dream is to put her in a cage.’

The quote reveals a desire for control masked as love. It reflects how some men admire a strong and independent women but feel threatened by that strength and seek to dominate it.

In today’s society this contradiction still exists. Many modern relationships begin with mutual admiration and equality, yet underlying gender expectations can still shape behavior. Some men are drawn to confident, ambitious women but later feel insecure by their independence. This often leads to control, ,limit or reshape their partner into a more “traditional” mold.

A real life example of this contradiction is when a woman in a relationship is discouraged from pursuing a career because it might “neglect the family” even though the man was originally impressed by her ambition. This mindset reinforces outdated gender roles where men are expected to lead and for women to follow.

The quote also makes you think if some people pursue relationships not for partnership but for power because true love should empower and support not dominate.

In healthy relationships today equality and respect are key. While traditional roles may still work for some , they must be freely chosen not forced. The quote is challenging us to reflect whether we love people for who they are or for how well they fit our image of control.
